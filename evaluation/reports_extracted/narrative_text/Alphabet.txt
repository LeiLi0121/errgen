Share pice catalysts in cloud & search

Company Overview: Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) is the holding company of Google, a tech giant offering a wide range of technological products in the likes of search engine, maps, calendar, mail, operating system. GOOGL also sells hardware products and online advertising services. GOOGL’s reporting segments are Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets. In terms of global market share for search engine, GOOGL has 91%.

Investment Overview: Generative AI will boost monetization across Google's ecosystem. In 3Q25, Google Services had a 38.5% operating margin with strong performance in Search (USD56.6bn), YouTube ads (USD10.3bn), and subscriptions/platforms/devices (USD12.9bn). Gen-AI tools and Shopping Graph's 35bn listings enhance ad monetization and drive online-to-offline commerce. With ~90% global search share, Services revenue is projected to grow at a 9% CAGR (FY26F–28F), maintaining digital ad dominance. GCP margins are improving rapidly. Cloud revenue grew 34% y/y to USD15.2bn in 3Q25, powered by strong uptake of AI Infrastructure and Generative AI among 70%+ of enterprise customers, yielding a 24% operating margin. Though below AWS and Azure, consensus sees GCP EBIT margins rising to 24–28% in FY26F–28F on USD77–128bn revenue (29% CAGR). A USD155bn backlog, converting 45–50% yearly, supports growth, while multi- year 2025 contracts monetize from 2026 amid elevated FY25–26 capex and continued capacity investments ahead. Ad-tech sentiment improved after Google halted the third-party cookie phaseout (April 2025). Chrome will introduce user-managed cookie settings, preserve data advantages and allow further Privacy Sandbox testing. Google advertising revenue was USD74.2bn in 3Q25, up from USD66bn in 3Q24. BUY with a revised TP of USD360 (prev USD323). Our TP is based on 33x (prev 30x) FY26F PE and FY26F earnings of USD135bn. The re-rating reflects: (i) rolling forward net profit base by 2 months, and (ii) higher target multiple of 33x vs previous 30x. Our multiple is at a 10% premium to peer average 30x as consensus expect GOOGL’s earnings to grow at a CAGR of 14% over FY26F- 28F vs peer CAGR of 12% as GOOGL is expected to gradually step up AI monetization in 2026 and rising margins of its cloud business.

Risks: Macroeconomic slowdown, weaker ad or cloud margins, shifting mobile search behavior, competitive ad-tech disruption, and adverse regulatory outcome. Sachin Mittal Analyst groupresearch@dbs.com
