Rich and diversified project pipelines to fuel future growth

Company Overview: Equinix, Inc. is a global data centre service provider catering to a diverse customer base across industries such as technology, finance, media, healthcare, and more. Equinix's operation reaches business hubs across the Americas, Europe, Asia-Pacific, and the Middle East. In addition to its core data centre leasing business, the company also provides services like digital infrastructure, data exchange, and private connections to hypercloud providers, such as AWS and Microsoft Azure.

Investment Overview: Global leader in the data centre industry. Equinix holds a c.21% in the global retail colocation market according to Synergy Research as of 2024, with 260 data centres in 33 countries across six continents. The company has 44%, 36%, and 21% of revenue coming from America, EMEA, and Asia-Pacific, respectively. It also has one of the most interconnected ecosystems, as it serves 10k+ customers and has 478k+ total interconnections within its facilities. Equinix partnered with Singapore's sovereign wealth fund- GIC - in July 2019 to develop hyperscale data centers under the " xScale " brand name. As of September 2024, Equinix had an xScale portfolio of 20 operational facilities across all three regions mentioned above. In 3Q24, Equinix announced plans to expand its xScale data centers through a USD15bn joint venture with CPP Investments and GIC, to build advanced, high-capacity facilities across U.S. campuses. Equinix added four new projects since 4Q24 across Frankfurt, Miami, Mumbai, and Washington, D.C., and opened 10 major projects, including capacity in Kuala Lumpur, Lagos, Manchester, Salalah, Santiago, and São Paulo. Equinix has a very good track record of growth and profitability. It has 20 years of consecutive revenue growth and sustained a 45%+ adjusted EBITDA margin in the past 10 years. Solid pipelines to fuel future growth. Equinix estimates that it will have 382k cabinet equivalent capacity by the end of FY24, compared to the current 364k total cabinet capacity. In terms of project pipelines, Equinix has more than 22,000 cabinets of retail capacity and more than 100 megawatts of xScale capacity to be delivered through the end of 2025. Diversified customer mix reduces exposure to macro volatility. Equinix's 10k+ customer base comes from a diverse set of industries, with 35%, 36%, and 29% of them from the cloud, enterprise, and network sectors, respectively. The company’s strength also stems from its unmatchable global reach, with 64% of its recurring revenue coming from customers deployed across all three regions. Its top 10 customers account for 17% of its MRR and top 50 customers account for 38% of its MRR. Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) increased by 3.8% y/y (flat q/q), in 1Q25, with the Americas region increasing by 4.1% y/y (flat q/q) and improvement across all regions. Moreover, the occupancy is bound to be very stable with very high lease terms – over 18 years on average and 83%+ of leases expiring after 2039. Rate BUY with unchanged TP of USD1,218. based on 29x 12-month forward EV/EBITDA, c.1 SD above 5-year historical average, as street expects Equinix EBITDA to grow by 11% CAGR over the FY24F-FY26F period and the continuous expansion of 56 major projects underway in 33 metros across 24 countries, including 12 xScale projects.

Risks: Foreign exchange rate risk. More than 50% of Equinix's revenue is contributed by the APAC and EMEA markets. Customer in-sourcing. Hyperscale cloud providers – the largest existing tenants of data centres – may opt to move all or significant data centre servers in-house.
