ConocoPhillips
Good times continue for the independent oil major 17 November 2025
Company Overview
ConocoPhillips is an independent exploration and production company,
headquartered in Houston, Texas and a key operator in the prolific Permian Basin in
the US. Total production for 2024 amounted to 1.99mmboepd, up 8.5% y-o-y. The
company’s production mix roughly consisted of 51% crude oil, 31% natural gas, 14%
NGL, and 4% bitumen.
Investment Overview
Leader in low-cost portfolio. ConocoPhillips (COP) continues to focus on
generating low cost-of-supply portfolio by transitioning its well drilling, casing,
and completion design. It has also undertaken property management to increase
efficiency, as well as acquired businesses like Concho Resources and Shell’s
Permian assets over the years to establish a leading position in the prolific
Permian basin. With record production of 1.99mmboepd achieved in 2024, COP
has guided for around 18–20% production growth in 2025 to 2.34–
2.38mmboepd, driven by organic growth as well as the completion of landmark
acquisition of Marathon Oil in late 2024. Production guidance has been
maintained at the end of 1H25, despite the planned USD1.3bn sale of Anadarko
Basin assets, closing early 4Q25.
Growing its core oil and LNG portfolios. 2025 capex budget has been moderated
to USD12.3-12.6bn from c.USD12.9bn but this is still higher than 2024 levels of
USD11.5bn, driven by continued growth in Alaska & Canada (USD2.0bn) and on
track to delivering low single-digit organic production growth. Total company and
Lower 48 production in 3Q25 reached 2,399mboepd (2Q25: 2,391mboepd) and
1,528mboepd (2Q25: 1,508mboepd) respectively, which reflects the Marathon
Oil integration. The LNG strategy continues to scale, adding 5 MTPA of offtake for
a ~10 MTPA portfolio, with North Field East, North Field South and PALNG on
track for first LNG in 2026, while LNG project capital has been cut to USD3.4bn
after a USD0.6bn credit against Port Arthur spend. Meanwhile, Willow (Alaska)
capex estimates was raised by USD1.5bn to USD8.5–9.0bn range due to inflation,
localised North Slope and marine cost escalation, which could be concerning to
shareholders, given the project is already pricey. Though first oil from Willow was
narrowed to early 2029, keeping the ~USD4.0bn FCF inflection intact, progress
will be keenly watched here.
Enhanced shareholder return guidance. COP has a strong balance sheet position
and has committed to return at least 30% of its cash from operations to
shareholders, with more usual 40-45% actual payout range. For 9M25,
distributions have totalled USD6.9bn, on track to beat 2024 level of USD9.1bn.
Distributions will roughly be split 40:60 between dividend and share buybacks.
This implies close to 9.5% total distribution yield at current prices. Given the
macro uncertainties, commitment to this level of distributions for the year will be
a strong support for the share price. Preliminary 2026 guidance points to 0–2%
underlying production growth, with capex at USD12bn (USD0.5bn below 2025
midpoint) and adjusted operating costs easing further (USD0.4bn lower y/y),
supported by lower major project capital, Marathon Oil synergies, and cost
reduction programme.
We have a BUY call on COP with a TP of USD118. COP’s share price performance
has been affected by recent volatility in oil prices. However, we reckon oil prices
will hold up in 2H25, and given the strong earnings delivery and shareholder
returns commitment, we reiterate our BUY rating on COP, with an unchanged TP
of USD118. Our TP is based on a forward P/BV multiple of 2.3x, which is at +0.5
S.D. levels historically.
Risks
COP is exposed to significant macroeconomic risks as earnings depend on the
price volatility of crude oil, natural gas and other refinery products. Further, COP
is exposed to significant legal and regulatory risk as energy industry is highly
regulated in different jurisdictions across the world. The Willow project in Alaska
has been mired in controversy owing to environmental concerns and it remains
to be seen whether the development can progress without delays in future.
