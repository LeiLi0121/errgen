Americn Express Co
Long-term growth remains intact 12 November 2025
Company Overview
New York-based American Express Company (NYSE: AXP) is a leading global
services company, primarily focusing on payment solutions and services. Among
its principal products and services are charge and credit cards, payment network
services and travel related services offered to consumers and businesses. AXP
ranksasthe fourth largestcard network globally by purchasevolume,behindChina
UnionPay, Visa and Mastercard, and boasted 140m cards in force worldwide as of
end 2023.
Investment Overview
Leading player in the high-end credit card market. American Express
continues to dominate the premium credit card segment, connecting
effectively with affluent consumers and SMEs through strong brand equity,
superior service, and a lucrative rewards ecosystem. The recent U.S. Platinum
card refresh has driven doubled account acquisitions and elevated
engagement, supported by the Amex Travel App and enhanced digital tools
for premium cardholders. Strategic partnerships with Toast, Resy, and Tock,
along with the launch of Amex Ads, strengthen customer experience and open
new monetization channels. Amex cards are now accepted at an estimated
160 million merchant locations worldwide, nearly 5x growth since 2017,
reinforcing the company’s global network and competitive moat.
Long-term growth remains intact. 3Q25 results demonstrated resilience, with
revenue +11% YoY to USD18.4 bn, EPS USD4.14, and billed business USD421
bn, driven by robust premium card spending and engagement. Expansion in
the high-end consumer and SME segments, adoption among younger
cardholders, and rising offline travel and lifestyle spending support long-term
targets of 10%+ revenue growth and mid-teens EPS growth. Operational
efficiency and disciplined marketing investments further enhance sustainable
profitability, while AXP’s #1 ranking in the J.D. Power 2025 US Credit Card
Satisfaction Study underscores strong customer loyalty and brand strength.
Caution remains on macro and competitive headwinds. Despite strong
execution, risks include potential slowdown in T&E and discretionary spending,
rising engagement costs, and intensifying competition from JPMorgan Chase
and Citi in the premium card segment. Emerging credit risks in lower-income
segments, though limited, and sensitivity to broader macroeconomic
conditions could temper near-term growth. Nevertheless, high-income
customer spending remains resilient, supporting billed business and fee
growth.
Maintain HOLD, TP raise to USD360. We maintain HOLD, raise TP to USD360,
based on a 21x FY26F forward PER (previously 17x), reflecting a 22% discount
to Visa. While long-term drivers - digitalization, premium engagement, and
network expansion - remain intact, higher expenses, credit risk, along with
lagging adoption in tokenization and digital payments, justify the discount.
Strong YTD share gains (~23%) suggest much of AXP’s premium card resilience
is already priced in, supporting a near-term HOLD stance.
Risks
Upside risks include stronger-than-expected consumer spending and
favourable economic conditions leading to improved credit quality.
Downside risks include increased competition or regulatory actions
concerning credit cards, particularly related to interchange rates, as well as
deteriorating credit quality.
